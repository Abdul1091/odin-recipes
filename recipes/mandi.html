Mandi

Description

Mandi has become one of Saudi Arabia's most cherished dishes. The name 'Mandi' comes from the Arabic word 'nada,' which translates to 'dew,' representing the tender, dew-like texture of the meat. This method of preparation, where the meat and rice are slowly cooked in a tandoor (a hole dug in the ground and covered), dates back centuries and illustrates the innovative adaptations of Saudi cuisine to the harsh desert environment.

It highlights the significant role that meat, especially lamb, plays in the Saudi culinary landscape.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of basmati rice
  • 1 kg lamb or chicken
  • 1 tablespoon of Mandi spice mix
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 tomatoes, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups of hot water
  • 2 tablespoons of ghee

Steps

    Step 1:
    Start by marinating the meat with Mandi spice mix and salt for at least 2 hours.
    Step 2:
    In a pot, saut√© the onions in ghee until golden. Add the garlic and tomatoes, and continue cooking for a few more minutes.
    Step 3:
    Add the marinated meat and brown on all sides.
    Step 4:
    Finally, add the rice and hot water to the pot, cover, and let it cook until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed.
    Step 5:
    Fluff the rice with a fork before serving, and Mandi is ready to be enjoyed.
