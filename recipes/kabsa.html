Al Kabsa

Description

Considered by many as the national dish of Saudi Arabia, Kabsa is a flavorful mixed rice dish typically made with basmati rice, meat, vegetables, and a blend of spices. Its variations from region to region underline the country's diverse culinary landscape and the adaptability of its cuisine.

With its deeply aromatic flavors and hearty combination of ingredients, Kabsa embodies Saudi Arabia's love for spice-infused, satisfying meals.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of basmati rice
  • 500g chicken or lamb
  • 2 large onions, chopped
  • 5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of raisins
  • 1/4 cup of sliced almonds
  • 4 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 carrots, grated
  • 2 dried limes
  • 1 tablespoon of kabsa spice mix
  • Salt
  • 4 cups of hot water

Steps

    Step 1:
    Start by saut√©ing onions in a pot until golden, add garlic, tomatoes, and carrots, and continue cooking for a few more minutes
    Step 2:
    Add the meat and brown on all sides. Then add the Kabsa spice mix, dried limes, and salt.
    Step 3:
    Add hot water to the pot, cover it, and let it simmer until the meat is tender.
    Step 4:
    Rinally, add the rice, raisins, and almonds to the pot, cover, and let it cook until the rice is tender and all the water is absorbed.
    Step 5:
    Fluff the rice with a fork, and Kabsa is ready to be served. Pro Tip: For a smoky flavor, add a piece of charcoal to the pot when adding the rice. Cover and leave it for a few minutes before serving.
